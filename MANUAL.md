# How to Use Vynta 📱

This is a quick guide on how to actually use the app. Nothing fancy, just what you need to know.

---

## First Time Setup

When you open Vynta for the first time, it'll ask you to sign in with your Google account.

Do it — it needs this to sync with Google Calendar. Without it, the scheduling part won't work properly.

That's the only setup step. After that you're on the Home screen.

---

## The Home Screen

This is where you'll spend most of your time.

At the top you'll see a greeting based on the time of day, your daily progress bar, and a Focus Mode card showing what's next on your list.

Below that is a date strip — tap any day to see what's scheduled for that day. Today is always highlighted.

The task list underneath shows your upcoming events for the selected day. Pending tasks have an empty circle on the left. Completed ones show a green checkmark.

**Tap any task** to see its full details.
**Tap the circle** on the left to mark it done. You'll feel a haptic when it completes.

At the bottom there are two navigation buttons — Home and History. That's it, simple.

---

## Creating a Task

Tap anywhere on the input area or the mic button to open the Create Task screen.

**The text field is the main thing here.** Just type what you need to do in plain English:

> *"Submit the ML assignment by Thursday evening"*
> *"Call the bank tomorrow at 11"*
> *"Finish reading chapter 4 before Sunday"*

The AI reads your message and figures out the task name, time, and date automatically. You'll see the detected context appear in the card above the input field as you type.

### Energy Level

Pick one of the three chips — Low, Medium, or High.

- **Low** — stuff you can do half-asleep. Admin tasks, organizing files, replying to basic messages.
- **Medium** — normal focus stuff. Meetings, emails that need thought, revision work.
- **High** — things that need your full brain. Coding, writing, studying, problem solving.

This helps the AI slot your task into the right part of your day.

### Voice Input

Tap the mic icon inside the text field to use voice instead of typing. Speak naturally, same as you'd type. Tap again to stop recording.

### Scheduling

Once you're done, tap **Schedule with AI** at the bottom.

The AI will find a time slot based on your energy level, existing calendar events, and your working hours from Settings. The task gets added to your schedule and synced to Google Calendar.

---

## History Screen

Tap the history icon in the bottom nav to get here.

At the top you'll see your productivity score for the current period — how many tasks you finished vs total.

Below that is your activity list. Every task you've completed, missed, or scheduled shows up here with a timestamp.

**Color coding:**
- 🟢 Green = Completed
- 🔵 Blue chip = Scheduled (upcoming)
- Grey refresh icon = Rescheduled

You can filter the list using the chips at the top — All, Completed, Scheduled, etc.

---

## Settings

Tap the gear icon on the Home screen to open Settings.

**Theme** — switch between Light, Dark, or follow system default. Dark is the default.

**Working Hours** — set what time your day starts and ends. The AI uses this to avoid scheduling tasks outside your active hours. Default is 5:00 AM to 10:00 PM, adjust it to your actual routine.

**Default Buffer** — gap between tasks. 15 min is the default. If you need more breathing room between tasks, bump it to 30 min.

**Google Calendar** — shows your connected account. You can sign out here if needed.

**Save Changes** button at the bottom only activates when you've actually changed something. Tap it to apply.

## Things to Know

- The app needs internet to use the AI scheduling feature. Everything else works offline.
- Google Calendar sync happens automatically after each task is created or completed.
- Tasks older than 30 days are kept in History but archived.
- If the AI misreads your input, you can always edit the task after it's scheduled by tapping on it.

This is still in testing so there might be bugs. If something isn't working:
